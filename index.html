Ise Grand Shrine

Ise Grand Shrine, officially called Ise Jingu, is a shrine dedicated to the celestial sun goddess Amaterasu(Amaterasu-Omikami). The area is roughly the same size as the center of Paris. More than 1,500 rituals are preformed yearly for the Imperial family, for world peace and the hugh harvest. Originally Amaterasu-Omikami was worshiped in the Imperial Palace until the reign of the 10th Emperor Sujin, who moved the Holy Mirror (the symbol of Amaterasu-Omikami) out of the Imperial Palace.
Then, during the reign of the 11th Emperor Suinin, the Emperor ordered his princess, Yamatohime-no-mikoto, to seek out the most appropriate place to permanently inshrine and worship Amaterasu-Omikami. After a long time of searching the princess received a revelation that the best place to worship nad inshrine Amaterasu-Omikami was in Ise. That was approximately 2,000 years ago.

Shibuya

Shibuya is one of the biggest and busiest parts of Tokyo. The Shibuya crossing is often used to represent the dynamic of Tokyo, but is also an excellent example of Shibuya itself: A trendsetting powerhouse for both style and clothes. Join the wandering crowd into the alleyways and discover hip new cafes or hangout at a bar or restaurant. Travel to Shibuya and experience the beating heart of Tokyo or appreciate the independent art scene.

Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle, or White Heron Castle, is considered Japans most spectacular castle. The castle is a national treasure and a world heritage site. Unlike many other japanese castles Himeji Castle has never been destroyed by war, earthquakes or fire and is known as one of Japan's twelve original castles.
Himeji Castle is at a strategic western point when considering Japan's former capital Kyoto. The first fortifications where finished in the 1400s, and the castle would continue to see fortifications by several different still. The castle as we see it today was finished in 1609. It is now made up of over eighty buildings spread across multiple baileys and they are connected by a series of gates and paths.
